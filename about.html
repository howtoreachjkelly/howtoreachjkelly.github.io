Where this started

Before the executive titles, I was a recording engineer — including a stretch at Skywalker Sound. That work taught me what pressure and craft actually feel like from inside a session, not from an org chart above it. My approach to leadership also traces back further, to a hospitality mindset I picked up from my father: the belief that the person running the room is responsible for making everyone else's work possible.

Structure sets you free

I've seen too many organizations lose creative momentum to the absence of structure — no agreed tools, no clear process, no consensus on how decisions get made. Structure isn't bureaucracy. It's what removes the friction that kills good work before it starts.
